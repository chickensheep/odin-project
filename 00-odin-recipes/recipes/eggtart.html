Eggtart :O

Description

Egg tarts are a delightful pastry that originated from Europe but have become iconic in many Asian cuisines, particularly in Hong Kong and Macau. These small, round treats feature a buttery, flaky crust filled with a smooth, creamy egg custard that is lightly sweetened and baked to perfection. The balance of the crispy crust and soft filling makes them irresistible, whether served warm or at room temperature.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Unsalted butter
  • Powdered sugar
  • Egg yolk

Steps

  1. Make the Crust: Combine flour, butter, and a pinch of salt. Add egg yolk and cold water to form a dough. Chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Shape the Crust: Roll out the dough, cut into circles, and press into tart molds. Trim excess dough.
  3. Prepare the Filling: Whisk eggs and sugar, then add milk and vanilla extract. Strain for smoothness.
  4. Assemble: Pour the filling into the crusts, filling them 80% full.
  5. Bake: Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 20–25 minutes until golden brown. Let cool and enjoy!
